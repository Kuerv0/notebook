The accounting cycle

Steps in financial accounting:
  - Step 1: Identify transactions
  - Step 2: Prepare journal entry
  - Step 3: Post to general ledger
  - Step 4: Unadjusted trial balance
  - Step 5: Post adjusting entries
  - Step 6: Adjusted trial balance
  - Step 7: Create financial statements
  - Step 8: Post closing entries

Debits and Credits misconceptions
  - Debits and credits are neither good nor bad
  - Debits and credits are not the same as adding or subtracting

Equity = owner's equity paid in - dividends paid out + retained earnings

Retained earnings = revenue - expenses

Equity = owner's equity - dividends + revenue - expenses

Dividends + expenses + assets = liabilites + owner's equity + revenue
                       Debits = Credits

Balance means "total at a point in time".

Definitions
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Financial accounting:
The process of identifying, recording, summarising & analysing an entity's
financial transactions & reporting them in financial statements.

Journal entry:
Record of a financial transaction.

Double entry accounting:
Core principle: stuff that a business owns = stuff that a business owes.

Assets:
Stuff that a business owns; things like cash and inventory.

Liabilities:
Debts to external parties.
  -> Obligations that you need to fulfill in the future

Net assets:
Extra money when a business makes more money than it spends.

Equity:
This represents your claim in the businesses net assets. If the businesses is
not doing so well, it represents the value that the project owes to you.

Accounting equation:
Assets = Liabilities + Equity

General ledger:
A database that stores a complete record of all accounts & journal entries.

Account:
A place where we record, sort & store all financial transactions that affect a
related group of items

Account types:
  - Assets
  - Liabilities
  - Equity
    * Revenue
    * Expenses
    * Withdrawals aka dividends

Revenue:
It represents the total amount of money earned by a company through selling
goods, providing services, or other operating activities.

Trial balance:
An accounting report showing the closing balances of all general ledger
accounts. List all of your accounts and their closing balances.
Cumulative total of all transactions affecting an account.

Adjusting entries:
Journal entries posted at the end of an accounting period to bring a business's
books in line with the accrual method of accounting.

Accounting rules:
There are standards such as
  - International financial reporting standards (IFRS)
  - Generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP)

Accrual method:
Revenue is recognised as it's earned expenses are recorded as they are
incurred.

Financial statements:
Accounting reports that summarise a business's activities over a period of
time.

  - Balance sheet: Snapshot of business's assets liabilites and equity at a
    single point in time which can teach the readers about your financial
    position.

  - Income Statement: Summarizes your businesses revenues and expenses over a
    period of time.

  - Cash flow statement: Summary of how cash and cash equivalents have flowed in
    and out of a business during a specific period of time.

Closing entry:
A journal entry that transfers balances from temporary accounts to permanent
accounts in the balance sheet.

Debits and credits:
Are words used to reflect the duality or double-sided nature of all financial
transactions

Economic benefit:
Is the potential for an asset to contribute either directly or indirectly to the
flow of an entity's cash. Credits represent the source and debits represent the
destination.

Account:
A place where we can record, sort and store all transactions that affect a
related group of items.

T-Account:
Visual representation of an account. It looks like a T.
